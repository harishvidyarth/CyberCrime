How FundTrail Works

The investigation workflow, end to end — for new officers

1 · Upload the bank file

When a complaint arrives, you receive an Excel file of bank transactions (the NCRP "complete trail" export). Drag it onto the upload box on your Dashboard. FundTrail reads every layer of transfers and registers the case under its acknowledgement (ACK) number. You can drop several files at once.

2 · Trace the money

Open the case to see the fund-flow graph: the victim's account on the left, and every onward hop the stolen money took, layer by layer. Click any account to see its transactions, KYC details, ATM/cheque withdrawals, and the state its bank branch is in.

3 · Hold & recover

Mark suspect transactions put on hold as banks confirm freezes. Record court order dates and refund amounts as money is recovered. The Refund Recovery page totals it all up — that recovery percentage is the unit's headline number.

4 · Generate letters

From the graph you can generate official notices to each bank (.docx or PDF) — suspect-account letters and victim-account letters with the case details filled in. Use the Letters (ZIP) button on any case to download them all at once.

5 · Keep the case diary

Every case has a Timeline: add dated investigation notes ("hold confirmed by Axis nodal officer"), and see the automatic record of uploads, holds and letters. Move the case from Open through Under Investigation to Closed as it progresses.

Good to know

  • Search (top bar) finds any ACK, account, transaction ID or bank in your cases.
  • Repeat accounts (admin) flags beneficiary accounts seen across multiple cases.
  • You only see cases you uploaded or were assigned. Admins see everything.
  • Sessions auto-lock after 30 minutes of inactivity — shared computers stay safe.
  • Enable two-factor authentication for your account.
  • Dark mode: the moon icon in the top bar.
